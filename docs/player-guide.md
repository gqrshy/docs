## Getting Started

### Requirements

Before you can join ranked battles, you need:

- **For Singles (1v1)**: At least 3 Pokemon in your party
- **For Doubles (2v2)**: At least 4 Pokemon in your party
- No banned items in your inventory (like Tera Orb or Dynamax Band)
- Pokemon that aren't banned in the format you want to play

### Opening the Ranked Menu

Simply type `/ranked` in chat to open the main ranked battle menu. From here you can:

- View your current stats (Elo, wins, losses)
- Join the matchmaking queue
- View leaderboards
- Check season information
- See battle format rules

## Understanding Formats

CobbleRanked offers two competitive battle formats:

### Singles (1v1)

- **Team Size**: Select 3-6 Pokemon
- **Battle Style**: Classic 1v1 Pokemon battles
- **Lead Pokemon**: 1 Pokemon leads the battle
- **Strategy**: Similar to Pokemon Showdown Singles or Battle Tower

Singles format is great for:
- Learning competitive battling
- Testing individual Pokemon strength
- Traditional Pokemon battle experience

### Doubles (2v2)

- **Team Size**: Select 4-6 Pokemon
- **Battle Style**: VGC-style 2v2 battles
- **Lead Pokemon**: 2 Pokemon lead the battle at once
- **Strategy**: Team synergy and support moves are crucial

Doubles format is great for:
- VGC (Video Game Championships) practice
- Team-based strategies
- More dynamic battles with switching and support

Each format has separate rankings, so you can compete in both!

## How to Queue for a Match

### Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Open the Ranked Menu**
   - Type `/ranked` in chat
   - The main ranked GUI will appear

2. **Check Your Team**
   - Make sure you have enough Pokemon
   - Verify none of your Pokemon are banned
   - Check that you're not carrying banned items

3. **Click "Join Queue"**
   - Select your format (Singles or Doubles)
   - You'll see a confirmation message
   - Music will start playing while you wait

4. **Wait for a Match**
   - The system searches for an opponent with similar Elo
   - You'll see your queue position and wait time
   - Matches typically found within 30-60 seconds

5. **Accept the Match**
   - When a match is found, you'll see a popup
   - Click the green checkmark to accept
   - Click the red X to decline (not recommended - causes penalties)
   - You have 30 seconds to accept

6. **Select Your Team**
   - Choose which Pokemon to bring into battle
   - Select your lead Pokemon (the one that starts)
   - Click confirm when ready

7. **Battle Time!**
   - You'll be teleported to the battle arena
   - Fight with honor and good sportsmanship
   - Win or lose, you'll gain experience and rewards

### Queue Tips

- The longer you wait, the wider the Elo range becomes (helping you find matches faster)
- Players within 100 Elo of you will match instantly
- After 30 seconds, the range expands gradually
- You can leave the queue anytime by clicking "Leave Queue"

## Battle Rules

### Level Adjustment

By default, all Pokemon are set to **Level 70** during ranked battles. This ensures fair competition regardless of training level. Your Pokemon return to their original level after the battle.

### Turn Timer

Each turn has a **30-second timer**. If you don't make a move in time:
- You'll receive a warning
- After 5 warnings, you'll forfeit the match
- Plan your moves quickly but carefully!

### Banned Items, Pokemon, and Moves

Each format (Singles and Doubles) has its own banlist. Common bans include:

**Typically Banned Pokemon:**
- Uber-tier legendaries (Mewtwo, Rayquaza, etc.)
- Mythical Pokemon (depending on server rules)
- Some overpowered Pokemon (check the in-game blacklist GUI)

**Commonly Banned Moves:**
- OHKO moves (Fissure, Sheer Cold, etc.)
- Evasion moves (Double Team, Minimize)
- Some problematic moves (Baton Pass, Last Respects)

**Commonly Banned Abilities:**
- Moody
- Shadow Tag
- Arena Trap

**Commonly Banned Items:**
- Bright Powder
- Lax Incense

Check the blacklist GUI in `/ranked` to see exactly what's banned on your server!

### What Happens If You Disconnect?

If you disconnect during a battle:
- You'll receive a "flee penalty"
- Your flee count increases by 1
- Penalties increase with each disconnect:
  - 1-5 flees: 5 minute queue ban
  - 6-10 flees: 15 minute queue ban
  - 11+ flees: 30 minute queue ban
- Flee count decreases by 1 every 24 hours (if enabled)
- You'll lose Elo as if you forfeited

**Avoid disconnecting!** Always try to finish your battles fairly.

## The Elo System

### What is Elo?

Elo is a rating system that measures your skill level. When you win, your Elo goes up. When you lose, it goes down. The system tries to match you with players of similar skill.

### Default System: Pokemon Showdown

By default, CobbleRanked uses the same Elo system as Pokemon Showdown:

- **Starting Elo**: 1000
- **Minimum Elo**: 1000 (you can't drop below this)
- **Elo Change**: Based on opponent's rating
  - Beating higher-rated opponents = more Elo gained
  - Beating lower-rated opponents = less Elo gained
  - Losing to higher-rated opponents = less Elo lost
  - Losing to lower-rated opponents = more Elo lost

### Elo Tiers (Approximate)

| Elo Range | Skill Level |
|-----------|-------------|
| 1000-1199 | Beginner |
| 1200-1499 | Intermediate |
| 1500-1799 | Advanced |
| 1800-2099 | Expert |
| 2100+ | Master |

### Inactive Decay

If you don't play for a while, your Elo may slowly decrease. This encourages active participation and ensures the leaderboards stay current.

- **Decay Rate**: 2 Elo per day of inactivity (default)
- **When It Runs**: 9 AM UTC daily
- **Purpose**: Keep rankings accurate for active players

### Daily Elo Gain Limit

Some servers limit how much Elo you can gain per day to prevent grinding. Check with your server admin if this is enabled.

## Seasons

### What are Seasons?

Seasons are competitive periods (typically 30 days) where you compete for leaderboard rankings. At the end of each season:

- Top players receive special rewards
- A new season begins (automatic rotation)
- Your Elo may reset (depending on server settings)

### Season Information

Check the current season with `/season`:

- **Season Name**: e.g., "Season 1"
- **Status**: Active or Ended
- **Time Remaining**: Days/hours until season ends
- **Leaderboard**: Your current rank

### End-of-Season Rewards

Top players in each format receive exclusive rewards:

**Example Rewards (may vary by server):**

**Singles:**
- 1st Place: 64x Diamond, 1000 coins, "Legend" title
- 2nd-3rd Place: 32x Gold, 500 coins, "Master" title
- 4th-10th Place: 16x Iron, 200 coins, "Elite" title
- 11th-25th Place: 32x Emerald, 100 coins

**Doubles:**
- Similar reward structure for Doubles format

Rewards are typically given as commands that grant you items, currency, and titles automatically!

## Tips for Success

### Team Building

- **Balance Your Team**: Include attackers, tanks, and support Pokemon
- **Cover Weaknesses**: Don't have a team weak to the same type
- **Check the Meta**: See what Pokemon top players are using
- **Practice Makes Perfect**: Try different strategies to find what works

### Battle Strategy

- **Know Your Matchups**: Understand type advantages and disadvantages
- **Predict Your Opponent**: Try to anticipate their switches and moves
- **Don't Rush**: Use your full turn timer to think through tough decisions
- **Save Your Win Condition**: Don't reveal your strongest Pokemon too early

### Climbing the Ladder

- **Play Consistently**: Regular practice improves your skills
- **Learn from Losses**: Watch replays and understand your mistakes
- **Study Top Players**: Observe what Pokemon and strategies they use
- **Stay Calm**: Tilt (frustration) leads to bad decisions
- **Finish Your Battles**: Disconnecting hurts your rating and gives penalties

### Good Sportsmanship

- **Be Respectful**: No trash talking or toxic behavior
- **Play Fair**: No exploits or cheating
- **Accept Losses Gracefully**: Everyone loses sometimes
- **Help New Players**: Share tips and strategies with beginners
